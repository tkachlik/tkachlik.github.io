Curiousity for the sake of curiosity is the best kind

Feeling smart? Great! Feels good, right? However, let's not get too comfy. Complacency is to be avoided at all costs!
Whether it is learning something new, trying to become an expert in a well-known field, or letting yourself still be surprised as a research veteran,
you always want to remember the key to being really smart, i.e. knowing you're not quite as smart as you'd like to think.
The more you know about how much you don't know, the more uncertain on the one hand, yet the more complex and wonderful on the other your world gets.
To put it poetically (and I am stealing this wisdom from an unknown author):
We can either be a big person in a small world, or have the courage to be a pigmy in an infinite universe.

Unlike the case of the Schrodinger's cat, a person cannot be simultaneously knowledgeable and ignorant of a topic.
Should you be so inclined, accept your role in the universe as an object too large for quantum states, and pick which one you want to be.
And most importantly, have fun! :)
